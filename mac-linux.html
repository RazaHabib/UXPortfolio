Case Study

Mac & Linux Security Redesign

Redesigned security applications for Mac and Linux to align with enterprise requirements and ensure cross-platform consistency.

Role: Lead Product Manager
Year: 2021
Platform: Mac & Linux Applications
Industry: Cybersecurity

Project Overview

While Windows security products were well-established, Mac and Linux offerings lagged in terms of features and user experience. Enterprise customers demanded parity across platforms for endpoint security.

The redesign brought Mac and Linux applications in line with enterprise-grade requirements, ensuring consistency, usability, and trust across all platforms.

My Role & Responsibilities

  • Product Strategy: Defined roadmap for cross-platform security parity.
  • Research: Interviewed enterprise IT teams to gather expectations for Mac/Linux support.
  • Design Leadership: Guided UX/UI design to ensure consistent workflows across platforms.
  • Execution: Oversaw development sprints and QA for Mac and Linux builds.
  • Metrics: Measured adoption, customer satisfaction, and reduction in support requests.

Key Challenges

  • Feature disparity between Windows, Mac, and Linux products.
  • Different OS constraints requiring tailored implementations.
  • Low enterprise trust in Linux/Mac security offerings compared to Windows.

Solutions & Impact

Introduced unified dashboards, consistent workflows, and enterprise-level controls. Delivered feature parity with Windows security products and improved customer trust.

Enterprise adoption of Mac/Linux products increased by 60%, support tickets decreased by 40%, and customer satisfaction rose to 87%.

Learnings & Future Directions

This project emphasized the importance of parity across enterprise platforms. Future improvements could involve centralized cloud-based policy management and predictive analytics for all operating systems.
